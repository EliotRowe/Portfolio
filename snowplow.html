Snowplow Obstacle Avoidance

Obstacle avoidance algorithm maximizing adhearance to a straight line path.

The Algorithm

  • Algorithm inputs include a list of target waypoints in order and a costmap of the area.
  1. Linear path between each waypoint and next is searched for costs higher than a specific threshold.
  2. First and last coordinates along the path above the threshold are marked as the front and back edges of the obstacle.
  3. Starting from midpoint of these edges, map is searched in a perpendicular line for a third point on boundary of the obstacle.
  1. Once the third point is found, the midpoint between it and each initial edge is calculated.
  2. From these midpoints, the map is searched in a perpendicular line to find more points on edge of the obstacle.
  3. Process is repeated recursively until the found point is close to the midpoint where there search started.
  • Method allowed the algorithm to follow the edge of complex obstacles, without having to determine the overall shape of the obstacle.
  • Specifying direction of perpendicular search allowed algorithm to always create turns in the same direction, beneficial for angled plow.

Snowplow Implementation

  • Ion's Autonomous snowplow competition held yearly in Minneapolis MN
  • Competition tasks teams with designing an robot to autonompusly plow a T-shaped region of snow
  • Obstacles are placed randomly in competition area; hitting obstacles incurs major score penalties
  • CWRU's competition robot OTTO placed first in the 2025 competition using this algorithmn for path planning
  • Minimized OTTO's deviation from its predetermined plowing strategy and allowed it to clear more than 95% of the snow from the competition area
  • Solved an issue observed with cost minimization algorithms where the robot would gradually move around the obstacle, minimizing distance traveled but leaving sections of uncleared snow
